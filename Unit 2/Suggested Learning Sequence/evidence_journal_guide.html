Evidence Journal Guide

How to keep a timestamped OneNote project journal for Unit 2

Start here: set up your journal section in OneNote Class Notebook in Lesson 1 of Week 1, before you build anything. It is your evidence of individual, ongoing development.

Why This Matters

Part A is judged Competent or Not Yet Competent, and Part B is marked out of 25. Both judgements rely on your teacher being able to see when and how you did the work, not just the finished product. A timestamped journal is your authentication evidence at every checkpoint.

Setting Up Your Journal

  • In your OneNote Class Notebook private section, create a page called Unit 2 Project Journal.
  • Add a new dated entry for every lesson — use Insert → Date & Time (or Alt+Shift+F) so each entry carries a real timestamp.
  • Paste or insert screenshots of your work-in-progress (code, diagrams, test output) directly under each dated entry.
  • Use the Student Scaffold to check what evidence each section of Part B will need later.

Using the Printable Evidence Log

Your teacher will also distribute an Evidence Journal template (Word document) via OneNote Class Notebook. Insert it into your journal section (Insert → File Printout) and fill in a new row every time you have evidence to record — it works alongside your dated OneNote entries, not instead of them.

Both checkpoints and the final Part B judgement depend on your journal showing genuine progressive development. A journal that appears all at once, undated, will not count as authentication evidence.
